Identity Experiment 01 — From Noise to Signal

Experiment summary: Over six weeks we narrowed a creator's voice to two themes instead of ten and switched the CTA from "follow" to "message about clarity". Outcome: fewer followers but a 4x increase in qualified client leads.

Step 0 — The diagnosis

The creator posted random tips across marketing, lifestyle, and productivity. Engagement was spread but meaningless. People liked but did not hire.

Step 1 — Pick a north star

We chose "identity systems for early stage creators". Every post had to pass the test: does this teach identity or is it distraction?

Step 2 — Reformat for proof

Each week we published one short case note, one thread that documented a micro experiment, and one reflective essay. Each post ended with one invite: message me about your identity problem.

Outcome

Engagement dropped 20 percent, reach remained flat, but messages that asked about clarity and budgets increased 4x. The creator closed two projects in month two.

Lesson

Audience that converts is narrower and clearer than an audience that applauds. Trade applause for alignment.
