How to Run Public Experiments That Build Trust

Public experiments are a brand's laboratory. They are faster than case studies and more credible than promises. Follow four rules.

Rule 1 — Make the metric meaningful

Pick one metric that matters to clients. Vanity metrics hide poor outcomes. Choose something that indicates value, for example "conversations that mention budget" or "email replies asking about process".

Rule 2 — Limit variables

Change one thing per experiment. If you change voice and distribution at the same time, you won't learn which tweak mattered.

Rule 3 — Publish the log

Short daily notes: what you tried, what you measured, what happened. Logs are shareable microproof that build narrative and trust.

Rule 4 — Extract lessons

After the window, write a short essay with findings and practical steps. This becomes evergreen article material.
